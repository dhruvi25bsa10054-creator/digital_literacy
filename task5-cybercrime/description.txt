While researching UPI/online payment fraud, I was most surprised by how easily scammers can trick even tech-savvy individuals using simple social engineering techniques, such as fake payment requests or QR codes. I didn’t realize that entering a UPI PIN for a “received” payment could actually transfer money to a fraudster. It highlighted how quickly digital transactions can be exploited when users are unaware of the correct procedures.

One habit I plan to change is being more cautious and verifying every payment request before approving it. I will also avoid sharing sensitive information, such as UPI PINs or OTPs, with anyone, even if they claim to be bank officials. Additionally, I will regularly educate myself on safe online practices and report any suspicious activity through official channels like the National Cyber Crime Portal (cybercrime.gov.in) or helpline 1930. This will help me stay safe and prevent potential financial loss.
